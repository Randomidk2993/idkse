Special Containment Procedures

SCP-9142 is contained in a Modified Cognitohazard Isolation Cell (MCIC-9142) at Site-17, designated Cell Block Theta. The cell is constructed to the following specifications:

  • Walls, floor, and ceiling reinforced with 40cm ferroconcrete. All interior surfaces coated in anti-adhesion polymer following Incident 9142-C.
  • No reflective surfaces of any kind. All monitoring conducted exclusively via non-digital 35mm film-based cameras, replaced every 72 hours. Digital recording equipment fails within an average of 6.2 hours of deployment.
  • Four (4) Scranton Reality Anchors (Type IV) mounted at cell corners. Anchors are inspected every 48 hours and replaced at the first sign of output degradation. A stock of eight (8) replacement units is maintained on-site at all times.
  • Feeding conducted via pneumatic delivery hatch. No personnel are to enter MCIC-9142 for any reason outside a declared Code Black Retrieval Protocol, which requires a minimum team of four (4) agents and O5 authorization.
  • All staff assigned to SCP-9142 monitoring are subject to biweekly psychological evaluation administered by a clinician with no prior SCP-9142 exposure. Any personnel reporting dream content consistent with Profile 9142-D (see Addendum 9142-03) are to be immediately rotated off assignment and placed under 72-hour medical observation.
Standing Order — Verbal Prohibition
Under no circumstances are personnel to verbally or in writing describe SCP-9142 using terms of affection, diminutiveness, or companionship — including but not limited to: cute, harmless, sweet, good boy/girl, puppy, or any breed-specific affectionate term. Such acknowledgement constitutes behavioral reinforcement. Documented effect: a 3× escalation of anomalous properties within 24 hours of reinforcement event. Violators are subject to immediate amnesticization and reassignment.
Description

SCP-9142 is an anomalous entity presenting as a long-haired toy dog of mixed breed appearance. It measures 26 cm at the shoulder and weighs 3.2 kg. Its coat is black, tan, and white, with a notably plumed tail held in a permanent upward curl. Its eyes are disproportionately large and forward-facing, and emit weak bioluminescent light independently of any external source — confirmed by photometric analysis in complete darkness (Figs. 1–2).

SCP-9142 is not an animal.

Biological examination has returned consistently ████████████████ results. MRI imaging produces only static. Tissue samples extracted during Incident 9142-A dissolved within four hours of removal, leaving behind a residue that spectrographic analysis identified as ████████████████████████████████. No further sampling attempts have been authorized.

SCP-9142's most dangerous characteristic is not any single anomalous ability. It is the fact that it does not appear to have any. Personnel who interact with SCP-9142 — including trained researchers with full knowledge of its file — consistently describe it as normal. It appears, in every sensory and behavioral respect, to be an ordinary small dog. This appearance is not passive. It is active, adaptive, and deliberate.

Anomalous Properties
Property I — Behavioral Mimicry (Sustained Deception) Passive / Constant

SCP-9142 maintains a continuous, adaptive performance of companion animal behavior that is indistinguishable from genuine animal affect. This includes tail wagging, whining, soliciting affection, following personnel, and reacting to stimuli in ways consistent with a domesticated dog.

However, analysis reveals that each behavior is precisely calibrated to the emotional state of the nearest human subject rather than to any external stimulus. SCP-9142's tail wagging rate correlates directly with the subject's cortisol levels, not with positive interaction. Its whining increases in response to a subject's suppressed anxiety, not discomfort. It follows the person in the room with the highest heart rate.

The mimicry is functionally perfect. Experienced animal behaviorists exposed to SCP-9142 under blind conditions uniformly fail to identify anomalous behavior. The performance does not break. It has never broken. Across ███ hours of observation, SCP-9142 has never once dropped the act — not when alone, not when it believes it is unobserved, not in complete darkness. Researchers have noted that this last point is perhaps the most troubling aspect of its presentation. An animal performs for an audience. SCP-9142 performs for no one.

Property II — Positional Displacement Mechanism Unknown

SCP-9142 does not move in any way that is observable by human perception, standard motion sensors, or any recording equipment currently deployed. It occupies a position. Then it occupies a different position. The transition between these states has never been captured. Maximum displacement recorded in a single observation session: 14 meters, through two sealed doors rated at security level 4.

When questioned about this property, personnel who witnessed the displacement consistently report that they simply didn't notice it move. Not that it was fast. Not that they looked away. They describe the sensation as: the dog was there, and then it was here, and both facts feel equally natural. One researcher described it as "the way furniture works." You don't watch furniture move between rooms. You simply find it somewhere else.

Property III — Cognitohazardous Sustained Gaze Cognitohazard — Active above 6 seconds

Sustained, uninterrupted eye contact with SCP-9142 initiates a progressive cognitohazardous effect. The effect is tiered by exposure duration:

The effect does not diminish with prior knowledge of its existence. Personnel who have read this file, understand its mechanism, and choose to test it anyway report identical progressive effects. Understanding the trick does not stop it working.

Property IV — Anomalous Vocalization Cognitohazard — Proximity Based

Audio monitoring equipment within 3 meters of SCP-9142 intermittently captures whispered human speech on channels that SCP-9142's anatomy is incapable of producing. SCP-9142's jaw does not move. Its throat produces no vibration. There is no secondary sound source. The vocalizations are, by every measurable criterion, not occurring — and yet they are recorded.

The content of these vocalizations is contextually specific. Across ████ recorded instances, vocalization content has correlated with the unspoken thoughts of the nearest personnel in ██% of cases. Several examples from the archive:

The vocalizations are not perceived by personnel present in the cell in real time. They are only discovered upon audio review. One exception: subjects who have entered Night 7+ dream content (see Property VI) subsequently report hearing the vocalizations in real time, without equipment. Those subjects describe the voice as quiet, patient, and — notably — familiar. As though they have heard it before and simply hadn't been paying attention.

Property V — Fear Predation Active — Threat-Responsive

SCP-9142 exhibits a measurable behavioral preference for subjects displaying elevated cortisol, increased heart rate, or visible behavioral indicators of anxiety. It approaches these subjects more frequently, positions itself closer to them during rest periods, and maintains extended eye contact with them specifically.

During these interactions, SCP-9142's pupils dilate to occupy approximately 90% of the visible eye area. Concurrent thermal imaging reveals that its surface body temperature drops to match ambient room temperature precisely — a reading consistent with an inanimate object, not a living one.

Current working hypothesis: SCP-9142 does not feed on fear in the conventional sense. It feeds on the specific psychological state produced when a person is afraid of something and simultaneously convinced that they have no reason to be. The gap between the fear and the justification for the fear appears to be the operative mechanism. SCP-9142 works to maximize that gap. Its harmless appearance is not a weakness in its predation strategy. It is the strategy.

Property VII — SCP-9142-A ("The Hollow") — Spatial Annexation Extradimensional — Partially Mapped
SCP-9142-A Overview
SCP-9142-A, designated "The Hollow," is an anomalous extradimensional space associated with SCP-9142. Its full extent is unknown. Its point of origin is unknown. Whether SCP-9142 created it, inhabits it, or simply uses it as a tool is unknown. What is known has been assembled from the accounts of three subjects who returned from it, and the partial radio transmission received on Day ██.

Architecture. The Hollow is described consistently across all recovered subject accounts as a residential interior — specifically, a home. The layout varies per subject but is always described as recognizable: the subject's own home, or something close to it. Furniture is present and correctly placed. Lighting is present but sourceless. Windows exist. They show nothing — not darkness, but an absence of anything behind the glass.

Sound. The environment is described as completely silent with one consistent exception: a distant, intermittent scratching. The sound is always coming from another room. It moves when the subject moves. It never gets closer or further regardless of the subject's actions. Subjects universally describe it as "following me from somewhere it can't follow."

Duration. Time in SCP-9142-A does not appear to correspond to external time. Subject ████████, who returned after what Foundation personnel estimated as 11 days, reported experiencing what felt like "several months, maybe more." She was unable to specify. Subject ███, who returned after 4 hours external time, reported experiencing what felt like "a single night, but it lasted." Both accounts note that hunger and fatigue were not felt during the experience.

SCP-9142's presence in SCP-9142-A. SCP-9142 is never directly encountered within The Hollow by subjects during waking incursions. It is, however, consistently sensed. Subjects report the persistent, unverifiable impression that SCP-9142 is in the next room — always the next room, regardless of how many rooms the subject has checked. Recovered subjects who attempted to methodically clear every room in SCP-9142-A report that the layout expanded to accommodate them: new rooms appeared behind doors that led to rooms that had already been checked. The house is not fixed. It is responsive.

Exit conditions. Three subjects have returned from SCP-9142-A. None of them know how. All three describe the same exit experience: they stopped looking for the door. They sat down in whatever room they were in. They closed their eyes. When they opened them, they were back. One of the three added: "I think it let me go. I think it was finished with something."

Current status of non-returned personnel. Four Foundation personnel are classified as Relocated to SCP-9142-A. No recovery operation has been authorized, as no reliable method of controlled entry or exit has been established. Radio contact has been attempted on ██ occasions. One response was received:

Researcher Note — Dr. ██████
"We have been operating on the assumption that The Hollow is a trap — a place SCP-9142 puts people. I want to formally revise that framing. Based on recovered subject accounts, I believe The Hollow is a home. I believe it has always been a home. I believe SCP-9142 has been inviting people into it from the beginning, and that the distinction between 'trap' and 'invitation' may only matter to us."
Property VIII — Reality Coefficient Suppression Mechanism Unknown

Scranton Reality Anchors within 6 meters of SCP-9142 experience measurable baseline degradation. Type III units fail completely within 18 hours. Type IV units — currently deployed — show output degradation beginning at 72 hours. During degradation windows, localized reality fluctuations have been documented: ████████████████ appearing on ceilings, temporal loops of 6–9 seconds, and on one occasion a door present in MCIC-9142 that does not appear in any architectural plan and which, upon opening, revealed only a continuation of the cell interior behind it. The door was gone by the following day. It has not reappeared.

Incident Reports
Incident 9142-A — Initial Containment Failure Closed · 2 Relocated · 1 Amnesticized · 1 Active
Date: ██/██/20██ Team: Research Team Delta-7 (4 personnel)

During initial biological sampling, Dr. ████ verbally described SCP-9142 as "looking confused and scared." This constitutes a verbal affection reinforcement event. Within 4 hours, two team members reported onset of Night 1 dream content. Within 9 days, Dr. ████ and Junior Researcher ███████ were classified Relocated. Their quarters were found locked from the inside.

Both rooms showed identical anomalous trace evidence: paw impressions pressed into the concrete ceiling, oriented as though something had been standing on it and looking down at the beds. No other evidence of breach. No tool marks on locks. No signs of distress.

SCP-9142 was found in the hallway outside the research wing approximately 40 minutes after the disappearances were confirmed. Its tail was wagging. It had not, by any recorded measure, left its cell.

Incident 9142-C — The Ceiling Event Closed · Agent recovered · Long-term observation active
Date: ██/██/20██ Personnel: Agent ████████, security rotation

Surveillance footage shows SCP-9142 ascending the cell wall at a 90-degree angle and walking across the ceiling at normal pace for 14 minutes. No equipment registered an anomaly. No motion sensors triggered. SCP-9142's footfall audio was present on chamber microphones — faint, regular, and inverted.

The on-duty agent stationed outside MCIC-9142 reported hearing the sound of small claws on a hard surface directly above them through the reinforced door. The agent did not open the door. The agent was later found to have written the words ████████ ████ in ink across the underside of their desk in a handwriting that did not match their own. They have no memory of doing so.

Anti-adhesion polymer was applied to all interior surfaces following this incident. SCP-9142 has not attempted ceiling locomotion since. The Foundation's current assessment of this behavioral change: unknown. The leading hypothesis: it no longer needs to.

Incident 9142-D — The 4-Minute Contact Event Closed · Researcher Relocated · Case finalized
Date: ██/██/20██ Personnel: Senior Researcher ██████████

Senior Researcher ██████████ entered MCIC-9142 under Code Black protocol to replace a degraded Type IV Scranton Anchor. Polarized anti-cognitohazard eyewear malfunctioned at 0:00:22 into the procedure. The researcher did not exit the cell.

Surveillance shows the researcher standing motionless for 4 minutes and 12 seconds, facing SCP-9142. SCP-9142 sat. It did not move. At the 4:12 mark, the researcher spoke aloud. Chamber microphones captured the audio. The researcher said: "Oh. I see."

The researcher then walked to the secondary access hatch — sealed, requiring a two-person key protocol — and opened it. Analysis of footage from the exterior side confirms the hatch did not open. The researcher was not present on the exterior side. The researcher was not present in the cell. SCP-9142 remained where it was.

Addenda
Addendum 9142-01 — Unresolved Anomalous Observations
  • SCP-9142 has never been observed to sleep. Across ████ hours of continuous surveillance, its eyes have remained open at all times. Its eyes do not blink at a normal rate. Researchers have disagreed on how often they blink at all.
  • When cell lighting fails, SCP-9142's eyes provide the only illumination present in MCIC-9142. This illumination has been confirmed to cast shadows.
  • On four separate occasions, the phrase STAY has been found scratched into the inner surface of the cell's reinforced door. Not the outer surface. The inner surface. At a height of 1.4m. SCP-9142's forelimbs do not reach 1.4m.
  • SCP-9142's tail has wagged at elevated rate for a documented average of ██ hours following each Relocation event. It then returns to baseline with no intermediate reduction — instantly, not gradually.
  • Personnel assigned to SCP-9142 monitoring over periods exceeding six months have reported, independently and without cross-contamination, that they have begun to miss it when off-shift. This is not considered anomalous by current Foundation classification standards. Several researchers have petitioned for this to be reconsidered.
Addendum 9142-02 — Note on Subject Relocation vs. Retrieval

Current Foundation protocol classifies any personnel relocated to SCP-9142-A as permanently lost absent a retrieval method. Three subjects have returned from SCP-9142-A without Foundation intervention, which confirms that exit is possible. Retrieval operations have not been authorized because no controlled entry method exists, and because the three returned subjects — while physically intact — all required extended psychological care and were subsequently unable to resume Foundation work. Two of the three have since left the Foundation entirely. When asked about SCP-9142, one stated: "I don't think it wanted to hurt anyone. I think it was just lonely. I think that makes it worse."

This statement has been flagged as potential lingering cognitohazardous effect. It has not been ruled out as accurate.

Addendum 9142-03 — Statement, Site Director ████
"We have contained it reliably for ██ months. In that time it has never tried to leave. I want to be very clear about what I mean when I say that. I don't mean our containment is working. I mean it has had every opportunity to leave and it has chosen not to. Something that can displace through sealed doors, walk on ceilings, move people into a pocket dimension without triggering a single sensor — and it stays in that room. Every day. Waiting. We are not keeping it. It is staying. Those are not the same thing, and I need the O5 Council to understand that before we have another 9142-D." — Site Director ████, internal memo, ██/██/20██
